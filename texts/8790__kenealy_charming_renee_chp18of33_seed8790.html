The Callanders had gone and the house was quiet. Stratheldon had not stirred from his rooms. The door opening upon the suite he occupied when in retirement was kept scrupulously locked. Mrs. Callander had made several attempts to penetrate to him, but she was, in every instance, inexorably denied. His lordship was indisposed. His lordship was much occupied with his writing. His lordship had been reading late into the night and was now resting, Morgan insisted, respectfully, but with his tall height plnated firmly in the doorway.
